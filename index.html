About

Hey, I'm Timothy.

I started in data the unglamorous way — as a Sales Operations intern wrangling Power BI dashboards and writing SQL for stakeholders who just wanted clear answers. I stayed for three years, grew into a full-time role, and learned that good reporting is mostly about asking the right questions before you write a single query.

Now I'm a Data Scientist at Nanyang Polytechnic, working in Organisational Transformation — a step up in scope and a chance to apply data to bigger, messier problems. My approach hasn't changed much: jump into the data, talk to the people closest to the problem, and ship something that gets 80% of the way there. Then iterate. Most of the value lives in those iterations.

Outside of work, I'm at the gym, behind a camera, or building iOS apps. I volunteer as a photographer for the National Day Parade — one of the more meaningful ways I've found to combine craft with something bigger than myself.

Data Infrastructure 2024 — 2026

Rebuilding an executive reporting platform

Align Technology (Invisalign) · 15 APAC markets

I inherited a regional executive dashboard that had become the central reporting layer for sales, marketing, finance, and operations leadership — and was quietly falling apart. The data model was bloated with overlapping tables, three different product mappings from different source systems, and duplicate measures producing conflicting numbers. Tracing the source of a single KPI could take an hour.

I rebuilt it. I consolidated redundant tables into curated Databricks views, unified the product mapping into a single reconciled source, and standardised multi-year FX rates across all revenue logic. In Power BI, I stripped out legacy measures, simplified Power Query transformations, and refactored every page to pull from validated, centralised measures.

I also implemented row-level security tied to user email and country code, with hierarchical visibility so country teams saw only their own market while regional leadership saw the full picture. Each functional team — sales, ops, finance, marketing — got their own dedicated views designed around the questions they actually asked.

Outcome

A single source of truth tracking $200M+ in regional hardware revenue. Adopted as the model architecture for future dashboards across the team.

Stack · Databricks · Power BI · DAX · SQL · Power Query

Data Modelling 2024 — 2025

A dynamic sales incentive engine for 15 markets

Align Technology (Invisalign) · 15 APAC markets

Quarterly sales incentive calculation across 15 APAC markets — each with its own rules, hierarchy, and reporting structure. Some markets calculated bonuses on direct sales, others on team performance with weighted targets. Sales reps could change managers mid-quarter, and revenue had to be attributed correctly to the right manager for the right period. Two parallel reporting structures ran in tandem under the same parent company, with a single sale potentially contributing to both.

I built it from the structure up. I aligned with regional sales operations leads to define a standardised hierarchy template, then onboarded 13 of 15 markets into the framework using structured Excel inputs that captured roles, parent-child relationships, regions, and quarterly targets.

In Power BI, I implemented a dynamic hierarchy model using PATH() and PATHLENGTH() with calculated columns to handle variable management depth. The row-level security system used timestamped hierarchy snapshots so that mid-quarter manager changes were respected — reps saw only their own data, managers saw their direct reports as of the right point in time.

Outcome

Validated by regional sales operations and adopted as the foundation for APAC incentive payouts. ~$8M in compensation accurately attributed to 550+ personnel across 15 markets in a single secure dashboard.
